True Fear Vs. Physical Fear

There's a difference?!

True Fear

This kind of fear warns you that danger is afoot. Imagine you're swimming and suddenly, WOOSH WOOSH! There's a shark swimming around you. You'll suddenly be really scared: you're full of adrenaline, your heart is pounding.... maybe you'll even scream. This fear is important as it triggers your instinct to protect yourself and keep you safe.

Physical Fear

This kind of fear is sensed when your body activates the fear response, but there's actually nothing that can harm you. "When a monster jumps out during a scary movie, you might experience the exact same physical response that the bear in your campsite triggered. The difference is that while a bear can hurt you, special effects cannot," says Daniel Wendler, PsyD.

Learn the difference between the two types of fear. This can help you to understand that while some situations may seem dangerous to you, they don't actually harm you at the end of the day! Scared of initiating a conversation with a (reasonably friendly) stranger? Recognize that this is only a physical fear, and know that you are completely safe.
